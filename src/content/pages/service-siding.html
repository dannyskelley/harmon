Why Insulated Siding Is Worth It for Michigan Homes

Standard vinyl siding does a great job protecting your home from the elements, but insulated siding takes it a step further. It adds a continuous layer of rigid foam insulation behind the siding panel, which reduces thermal bridging through wall studs and can meaningfully lower your heating and cooling bills. For Michigan homeowners, the long-term energy savings often justify the modest upfront cost difference.

Frequently Asked Questions: Michigan Siding

How long does new siding last in Michigan?

Quality vinyl siding typically lasts 20 to 40 years in Michigan's climate. Insulated vinyl and composite siding can last even longer with minimal maintenance. Proper installation is key to maximizing lifespan.

What is insulated siding and is it worth it?

Insulated siding has a continuous layer of rigid foam bonded to the back of each panel. It reduces heat transfer through wall studs and can lower energy bills. For Michigan homeowners, it often pays for itself within a few years.
