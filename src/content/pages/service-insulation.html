Michigan Insulation Contractor — Reduce Energy Bills & Improve Comfort

If your Michigan home is hard to heat in winter or cool in summer, or if your energy bills seem unusually high, inadequate insulation is often the culprit. Infinity Home Solutions provides professional insulation installation for attics, walls, crawl spaces, and basements across Metro Detroit and Southeast Michigan.

Michigan's climate — with winters that regularly dip below 0°F and humid summers — demands exceptional insulation performance. Our team installs the right materials in the right places to give your home maximum thermal protection year-round.

Our Insulation Services

Attic Insulation

Attic insulation is the single highest-impact upgrade most Michigan homes can make. We install blown-in and batt insulation to bring your attic to Michigan Energy Code standards and beyond. Proper attic insulation can reduce heating and cooling costs by 15–25%.

Wall Insulation

Exterior wall insulation dramatically reduces drafts and improves comfort throughout your home. We can install insulation in exterior walls during a siding project or as a standalone upgrade using blown-in techniques.

Crawl Space Insulation

Cold floors? Moisture issues? Crawl space insulation and vapor barriers keep your floors comfortable, prevent moisture damage, and improve indoor air quality. Essential for Michigan homes with crawl spaces.

Air Sealing

Air sealing goes hand-in-hand with insulation. We identify and seal gaps, cracks, and penetrations that allow conditioned air to escape and outside air to enter — multiplying the effectiveness of new insulation.

Insulation Removal

Old, damaged, or contaminated insulation removed safely and efficiently. We prep the space properly so new insulation performs at its best.

Insulation with Siding

Pairing new siding with insulated siding panels or wall insulation is one of the best ways to upgrade your home's energy performance in one project. Ask us about bundled pricing.

Insulation Types We Install

  • Blown-in fiberglass — ideal for attics and existing wall cavities
  • Blown-in cellulose — eco-friendly option with excellent thermal performance
  • Fiberglass batt insulation — for open wall and floor joist cavities
  • Rigid foam board — used under siding for continuous insulation
  • Vapor barriers — crawl space moisture control

How Proper Insulation Saves Michigan Homeowners Money

According to the U.S. Department of Energy, homeowners can save an average of 15% on heating and cooling costs by properly air sealing and insulating their homes. For a Michigan homeowner spending $2,000/year on heating and cooling, that's $300 or more back in your pocket every year. Over a 10-year period, that's $3,000+ in savings — and new insulation typically pays for itself in 3–5 years.
