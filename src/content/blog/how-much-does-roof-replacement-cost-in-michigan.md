## What Affects the Cost of a New Roof in Michigan?

Several factors move the price up or down:

**Roof size and pitch.** Bigger homes cost more. Steeper roofs take longer and require more safety equipment, which adds labor cost.

**Shingle quality.** We install Owens Corning Duration architectural shingles as our standard, a premium, highly rated product. If you want a step up, designer shingle lines are available at higher price points.

**Decking condition.** If your plywood decking is rotted or damaged, we repair it. We only know the full extent until the old shingles come off, but we tell you upfront and charge fairly for any extra material needed.

**Number of layers.** Michigan code allows two layers of shingles. If you already have two, both layers need to come off before the new roof goes on, which adds labor time.

**Chimneys, skylights, and penetrations.** Every cut and flashing point takes time. More of them means more time on the job.

## Should You Get Multiple Quotes?

Yes. We always encourage Michigan homeowners to get a few quotes before choosing a contractor. When comparing, make sure you are comparing the same scope of work. Some quotes leave out items like ice and water shield, new drip edge, or decking repair. Cheaper is not always a better deal if the scope is different.

What to look for in any quote:

- Full tear-off included (not a re-roof over existing shingles)
- Ice and water shield on eaves and valleys
- Manufacturer warranty on shingles
- Labor warranty from the contractor (ours is 10 years)
- Contractor is licensed and insured in Michigan
